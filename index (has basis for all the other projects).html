September 2023 -- April 2024

Senior Design Project
"Fight Flight", The drone-fighting project

Electrical Testing Engineer and Mechanical designer.

Heavily inspired by drone fights that clash and break each other, as well as flight simulator video games, "Fight Flight" is the software part of our project which aims to use the cameras on the FPV drones to have them fight digitally, through a computer, to ensure that people can practice their drone fighting and drone combat skills without having to actually break the drone. In simpler words, it teaches users how to aim drone weapons better and dodge enemy fire while pilotting the drone

I primarily served as a testing engineer and Mechanical Designer for this project. Designing the numerous chassis in SOLIDWORKS, I was eventually able able to mount all of the parts on the drone, get it to fly, and even had it survive a +200ft free fall without any visible or technical damage to components. Under my supervision, the drone was able to meet and surpass the original minimmum design specs (fly 17 meters/s, atleast 200ft vertical and 60 ft horizontal distance from controller without losing camera quality, etc) through numerous tests designed and carried out by all of us in the team. Played a great part in designing the custom transmitter (controller) for the drone, which was needed to activate the "guns" in augmented reality. Designed a custom PCB and controller-backplate that'd universally fit drone transmitters. If youre interested in hearing more about the project and its development, the project report file is avaliable through the following button in Google Drive.

February 2023 -- June 2024

AIChe
Chem E. Car -- The Chemical & Electrical Car Project

Electrical Engineering & Testing Lead

Chem E. Car is a competition hosted by the American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChe), which aims to connect chemical, electrical, and mechanical engineering disciplines in order to create a small car to compete.

Collaborated with two electrical engineers to pick some of the Printed-Circuit-Board (PCB) components seen above, primarily the transistors to lower gate opening time as well as limit voltage going to system from +20V down to 5V (transistor is labeled U6, others avaliable in second PCB image) and microcontroller (Arduino Nano) that'd act as the brain of the whole vehicle.

Coordinated, evaluated, and managed performance tests on the car, “Curious George”, to ensure all systems (stopping, drive, and checmical injection) functioned to specification. Corrected major design flaws in the electrical system for “Curious George”, ran the required distance of 15 to 25 ft 2 times over with all systems and extra weight attached.

Fabricated customized enclosures in SolidWorks to house exposed electrical components on the top part of the car.

MIPS CPU Design

A school project where students design a 16-BIT CPU in MIPS architecture. The CPU is programmable in the MIPS assembly language.

Implemented individual but critical parts of the architecture to ensure that the CPU is capable of Simultaneous Multi Threading (SMT), which makes the CPU capable of working multiple instructions at the same time. Allows CPU to have no gaps in between operation stages.
