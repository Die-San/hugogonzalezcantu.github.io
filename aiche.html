Chem-E. Car Project

Project Objective

Chem E. Car is a competition hosted by the American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChe), which aims to connect chemical, electrical, and mechanical engineering disciplines in order to create a small car to compete.

Involvement

  • Collaborated with two electrical engineers to pick some of the Printed-Circuit-Board (PCB) components seen above, primarily the transistors to lower gate opening time as well as limit voltage going to system from +20V down to 5V (transistor is labeled U6, others avaliable in second PCB image) and microcontroller (Arduino Nano) that'd act as the brain of the whole vehicle.

  • Coordinated, evaluated, and managed performance tests on the car, “Curious George”, to ensure all systems (stopping, drive, and checmical injection) functioned to specification. Corrected major design flaws in the electrical system for “Curious George”, ran the required distance of 15 to 25 ft 2 times over with all systems and extra weight attached.

  • Fabricated customized enclosures in SolidWorks to house exposed electrical components on the top part of the car.
